Wikipedia - The Free Encyclopedia

Wikipedia is one of the most remarkable and influential websites on the internet today. It has revolutionized how people access information around the world.

Launched in 2001 by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger, Wikipedia is a free online encyclopedia that anyone can edit and contribute to.

Unlike traditional encyclopedias, Wikipedia is dynamic and constantly updated by a global community of volunteers.

The site offers articles on nearly every topic imaginable, from science and history to pop culture and technology.

One of its strongest features is the collaborative editing system that allows for quick updates and improvements.

With millions of articles in over 300 languages, Wikipedia is accessible to users all over the world.

Its open-source nature has inspired the development of other collaborative knowledge projects.

Wikipedia has become a primary reference point for students, teachers, researchers, and casual learners.

Despite concerns about accuracy, studies have shown that Wikipedia can be as reliable as traditional encyclopedias.

The site is supported by the non-profit Wikimedia Foundation, which relies on donations to keep the platform ad-free and accessible.

It has also introduced sister projects such as Wikimedia Commons, Wikibooks, and Wikivoyage.

Wikipedia emphasizes neutrality and verifiability, encouraging contributors to cite reliable sources.

It serves as a great example of what a decentralized and democratic knowledge platform can achieve.

Many institutions and scholars now consider Wikipedia as a valuable starting point for research.

With a vast range of contributors, the content often reflects a balance of global perspectives.

Wikipedia has even been preserved in various offline formats for use in remote or disconnected areas.

Its influence extends beyond education — it's also part of digital activism, journalism, and policy discussions.

Schools and universities around the world use Wikipedia to teach students about collaboration and citation.

The site continues to evolve with new technologies and efforts to fight misinformation.

Ultimately, Wikipedia has become a symbol of open knowledge and collective intelligence in the digital age.
